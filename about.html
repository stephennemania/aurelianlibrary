Bibliotheca Aureliana bears the name of the late Roman Emperor Aurelianus, who single handedly saved the Roman Empire from a complete collapse in the third century, and it was created on the purpose to promote the Latin language and to help to maintain alive the European indigenous culture. Inspired by the dream of a revival of the European world, this website has the intention to display bilingual versions of some - hopefully all, someday - of the old Latin texts.

This website collects its texts mostly from the Public Domain and other major websites dedicated to Latin language; when possible, sources will be displayed. The Bibliotheca Aureliana is not intented to teach Latin nor to make academic research on the texts published, but solely to help to preserve old Latin texts and keep alive the interest for it. Critical apparatus or e-books, if and when they appear, will be an extension of this website's purpose, not the core of it.
